Parkville is known for its leafy streets, heritage houses and the University of Melbourne's bustling campus and residential colleges.

Royal Park

Parkville is home to major hospital, research and institutional facilities including the Royal Melbourne and Royal Children's hospitals.

Royal Park - the municipality's largest open space - can be found here along with one of our major attractions, Melbourne Zoo.

Parkville was the site of the 2006 Melbourne Commonwealth Games Village housing athletes and officials during the event. Now called Parkville Gardens, the site has been transformed into an integrated, socially inclusive community with private and social housing. It was designed to be part of a legacy of lasting social benefits left by the games.

Parkville Gardens includes Mercy Place, a 140-bed aged care residential facility offering dementia-specific care. There are also 52 independent living apartments to house the aged care community.
