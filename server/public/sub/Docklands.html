Docklands became part of the City of Melbourne municipality in July 2007. The suburb's 200 hectares of land and water are on Victoria Harbour, west of the city centre.

Dock of Docklands

The inclusion of Docklands in the City of Melbourne doubled the size of Melbourne's central city and returned a significant area of waterfront to the city.

The area is close to Melbourne's main transport networks, including Southern Cross Station, and is well known as the home to Marvel Stadium and many modern buildings, public spaces and artworks.

From the original development at New Quay, featuring apartments, restaurants and marina, Docklands has grown as a waterfront precinct offering a mix of inner city living, shopping, business, parks, entertainment, dining, sporting events, community events, arts and culture.

Docklands is the national headquarters for a number of major businesses including the National Australia Bank, ANZ, Myer, AXA, Bendigo Bank, Medibank Private and the Bureau of Meteorology.

It's estimated that by around 2040 Docklands will be a residential area for over 31,361 people and 92,386 workers. Additionally, Docklands is planned to attract 20 million visitors each year.

Docklands is also home to some of our biggest events with sporting and recreation venues in the area. We manage the Melbourne City Marina - Melbourne's largest visitor berthing facility, the Yarra's Edge Marina and the day-to-day operations of the waterways, allocation and licensing of berths and marina leases. Additional information on Docklands waterways is available at boating and waterways.
